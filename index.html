The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization

The National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC) in Cairo is Egypt’s first museum dedicated to showcasing the entirety of Egyptian civilization, from prehistoric times to the modern era. It is located in Fustat, Old Cairo, overlooking Ain El-Sira Lake, and is most famous for its Royal Mummies Hall.

The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) in Giza is the world’s largest archaeological museum dedicated to a single civilization, housing over 100,000 artifacts including the complete Tutankhamun collection

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo

The Egyptian Museum in Cairo (Al-Matḥaf al-Miṣrī) is Egypt’s oldest museum of antiquities, and houses one of the world’s most important collections of Pharaonic artifacts, including treasures from Tutankhamun’s tomb.
